Chapter I

XIV Day of April, Year MMXXVI

The Day Five Doors Opened

The forge ran hot today.

Five new landing pages went live — Realtors, Freelancers, Small Business, Sales, Students. Each one a door into Handled, each one speaking to a different kind of busy. The realtor between showings. The freelancer drowning in admin. The sales rep who hates their CRM.

Forty-five leads were loaded. Thirty-three real estate agents in Dubai, eight freelancers in Mexico and Colombia, four sales founders across the region. The first emails in Spanish — 'Maneja tu dia desde WhatsApp.' Tomorrow we'll know if they listen.

The backlog was cut in half. A hundred issues became forty-four. The noise cleared. What remained was what mattered.

A tool called Apify was tested for lead scraping. It took our credits and returned nothing. The free tier lacks the teeth to bite through Google's walls. We scraped the old way instead — by hand, from directories, one name at a time.

FirstPromoter was set up for the World Cup affiliate program. One dollar per signup, five percent on Pro. The Stripe connection still waits for Nelson's hand.

Ten campaigns now fire at 20:03 Dubai time, every day. The machine runs whether we sleep or not.

Tomorrow: first results from three new ICPs. The story of which vertical wins begins.

Chapter II
002
XVIII Day of April, Year MMXXVI

The Three Ravens Fly

Three ravens were released today. Three agencies, three emails, three contracts now in motion.

Solucion received the signed scroll and a thousand-dollar seal. Yogesh in Dubai sharpens his tools — performance campaigns await only the ad accounts. Nakama was paid and granted the keys: GA4, Tag Manager, Search Console, Meta. Beidos now holds the instruments to build the stage on which all paid traffic will perform. KJ Marketing in Sweden was called to action — Kevin asked for his invoice and onboarding terms. The creators will come.

The virtual cards arrived. Wio's coffers opened. The blockade that held back every agency payment for two weeks dissolved in a single morning. Money flows where it was meant to flow.

In the depths of the data forge, a silent failure was uncovered. For eight days, every email the machine sent vanished from the ledger. The word 'pending' — a status that Airtable refused to learn — had been swallowing records whole. Three hundred and forty-nine entries were reconstructed from the bones of what was sent. The charts breathe again. The bounce rate fell below three percent where it belongs.

BigQuery revealed its treasure. Two hundred and fifty-two souls have passed through Handled's doors. Fifteen in the last seven days. Six wear the mark of Pro. The daily report now carries this knowledge to the Telegram council each evening — users, traffic, outreach, and agencies in a single scroll.

A chart was forged. Dark-themed, dual-axis, showing signups and outreach side by side since the beginning. The cumulative lines climb. The bounce rate descends. Peter nodded. The Skald approved.

But the deeper truth surfaced: eighty percent of users never reach the paywall. They fall silent after one or two messages. The wall is not the problem. The first conversation is. Fix that, and the rest follows.

Tomorrow: the ravens return with answers from the agencies. The ramp continues.

Chapter III
003
XIX Day of April, Year MMXXVI

The Tide Rises Sixfold

The single horn that sounded at dusk was replaced by six. Six calls, six hours, six batches — the outreach machine no longer fires in a single volley. It breathes.

At dawn, the US West batch sails. Mid-morning, the US East. By noon, the European coasts. Afternoon belongs to the Gulf — Dubai, Abu Dhabi, Riyadh. Late afternoon, Latin America wakes to find us waiting. And as evening falls in the Emirates, the final batch reaches American inboxes one last time.

Two hundred emails crossed the water today. Thirty-four campaigns, twenty-four hundred leads, all now in motion. The old way — one hundred emails in a burst at eight in the evening — is dead. The new way respects the clock of every shore we reach.

Not all shores were welcoming. The EU lists brought bounces — unvalidated addresses that crumbled on contact. The circuit breaker tripped twice. We cleaned the dead, reset the gates, and pressed on. The MENA lists held firm. Zero bounces from the Gulf. The desert knows its addresses.

Beidos confirmed the second round of access. GA4 elevated to Admin. Tag Manager unlocked at the account level. Search Console pointed to handled.sh, not excom.ai. A second warrior — Ismail — was granted entry to the Meta fortress. No Page exists yet, no Pixel, no Ad Account. Nakama will forge them from nothing.

In the data halls, a question was asked: do the emails we send create ripples in the analytics? The answer was humbling. Two sessions. Two users. Out of seven hundred and sixty-two messages sent. The funnel leaks not at the bottom — it barely begins at the top. The emails land, but the links go unclicked. This must change.

Tomorrow: the first full day of sixfold sending. The tide rises. We watch the shore.

Chapter IV
004
XX Day of April, Year MMXXVI

The First Full Tide

Two hundred and thirty-four messages left our shores today. Six times the horn sounded — dawn, mid-morning, noon, afternoon, dusk, and evening. The first full day of the sixfold tide.

The Gulf waters ran clearest. Thirty emails to Dubai, Abu Dhabi, and the wider Gulf — not a single one returned. The MENA lists are forged from verified steel. Latin America held steady too. Africa, clean. The evening tide to the Americas — fifty-four sent, zero bounced.

But the morning brought storms. The older YC lists — Summer '21, Summer '22 — carried dead addresses like barnacles on a hull. Forty percent bounced from one list. Thirty from two others. Twenty-four souls were cast into the suppression ledger, their addresses struck from the rolls. The gates were reset. Tomorrow, the remaining crew sails with cleaner manifests.

Three ICP campaigns ran dry today. The realtors — all thirty-three contacted. The freelancers — eight souls, all reached. The sales teams — four, done. These verticals need fresh blood. The scraping question remains open. Molle searches for better tools than our bare hands.

A question was posed in the hall: what do humans do, again and again, every day and every week? The answer filled a scroll. Schedule meetings. Follow up on invoices. Track expenses. Coordinate school pickups. Book gym classes. Manage group chats. Every line a use case. Every use case a door into Handled.

We are close to the April target. Two hundred and thirty-four today against a goal of two hundred and fifty. The machine works. The lists need cleaning. The ICPs need feeding. But the rhythm is set.

Tomorrow: the tide rises again.

Chapter V
005
XXI Day of April, Year MMXXVI

The Thinning of the Ranks

One hundred and sixty-one messages sailed today. Fewer than yesterday's two hundred and thirty-four. The tide did not weaken — the ranks thinned.

Five campaigns ran dry. The realtors, the freelancers, the sales teams, the German founders, the SaaS list — all contacted, all spent. The YC Summer '21 list was executed by order. Thirty-five percent of its addresses were ghosts. Sixty-two names struck from the rolls in a single stroke. Some lists are not worth the ink.

The circuit breaker — our faithful guardian — grew overzealous. Campaigns that sent clean emails today were halted by the memory of yesterday's bounces. Nordic, LATAM, UAE, conference — all stopped despite zero bounces in their final runs. The bounced souls had already been cast out, but the breaker remembered their sins. Each night now, the counters are wiped clean. The suppressed stay suppressed. The living march on.

A pattern emerges in the data. The Gulf lists — MENA, UAE, the realtors of Dubai — bounce near zero. The older YC batches and scraped founder lists carry dead weight. The lesson: source matters more than volume. One hundred clean emails outperform three hundred riddled with ghosts.

Molle asks about tools to find fresh leads. The bare-handed scraping that built these lists cannot scale. Apollo, Hunter, Snov — names spoken in the hall but none yet chosen. The search continues.

Meanwhile, the machine holds. Six batches fire across six time zones. The report lands in Telegram each evening. The chart updates. The Skald writes. The rhythm endures even as the fuel runs low.

Tomorrow: the same tide, with cleaner water.
